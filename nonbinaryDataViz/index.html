"Diversity" in college has less to do with black students than many would think

Boston's elite​ campuses reflect new levels of diversity, attracting students from all over the world. But take a closer look --
The black students are ​- and always have been - ​hard to find.
The black students increased from 5% to 8% in the past 20 years across Boston metro area.

In 1980 at Boston University, black Americans were 4 percent​ of the student body -- and it’s still the same

At Harvard University, they were 5 percent - and the latest data shows no change

At Northeastern University, the percentage of black Americans is virtually unchanged from 4 to 5 percent in more than three decades.
